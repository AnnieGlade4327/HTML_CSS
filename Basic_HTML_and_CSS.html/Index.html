The Hydrocarbon Highway, Transwest Express powerline and Uintah Basin Railway are all current threats to this amazing and vital migratory and endemic wilderness corridor.

It is important to remember that the reasoning used by the recent judge that shut down the Uintah Basin Railway right-of-way and federal permitting for the project in August (of 2023) is largely in part due to concerns around the railline meeting of up old railine infrastructure, worried the solidified or high wax crude would be transported along this section which runs along the mighty Colorado River. While this is not the case, the reasons we should be sworking to stop these three projects have much more to do with it being a vital migratory corridor in addition to a very important watershed to not just Utah and Northwestern Colorado, but the entire four corners regions of the United States. The loss of these vital old growth forests and woody shrubs, wildflowers and other pertinent endemic and native plantlife throughout the Ashley National Forest, public,private, and Ute lands surrounding the High Uintahs of Eastern Utah, mass deforestation and clear cutting has already had a detrimental affect on our climate and airquality in recent years.
