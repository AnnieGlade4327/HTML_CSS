The Hydrocarbon Highway, Transwest Express powerline and Uintah Basin Railway are all current threats to this amazing and vital migratory and endemic wilderness corridor.

It is important to remember that the reasoning the judge shut down the Uintah Basin Railway proposal recently is largely in
part due to concerns around the railline meeting of up old railine infrastructure, worried the solidified or high wax crude
would be transported along this section which runs along the mighty Colorado River. While this is not the case, the reasons
we should be sworking to stop these three projects have much more to do with it being a vital migratory corridor in addition
to a very important watershed to not just Utah and Northwestern Colorado, but the entire four corners regions of the United States.
The loss of these vital old growth forests and woody shrubs, wildflowers and other pertinent endemic and native plantlife throughout the Ashley National Forest, public,private, and Ute lands surrounding the High Uintahs of Eastern Utah, mass deforestation and
clear cutting has already had a detrimental affect on our climate and airquality in recent years.
We do not have anymore Understory to spare in this region after the last 6 years of a neo oil-boom post the 2017 environmental rollbacks and gutting of wilflife protections here in the state of Utah.
